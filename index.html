01
Exterior

The
Facade

Set among mature oaks steps from the water, the residence announces itself with restraint. Cedar, stucco and steel — materials chosen to belong to this landscape, not impose upon it.

Day · Natural Light
6 Harding Lane · Sag Harbor

Built for
a life well-lived

Every room was designed around a single question — how does this space feel to inhabit? Not to photograph, not to impress. To live in, season after season, on the East End of Long Island.

02
Entry

Arrival
Sequence

A covered pergola walkway flanked by native grasses and climbing vines. The approach is deliberate — a transition from street to sanctuary, from pace to stillness. Before a door is opened, the house has already made its case.

03
Living Room

Great
Room

Vaulted white oak ceiling, linear fireplace, floor-to-ceiling glass folding open to the garden. The room was conceived not as a showpiece but as a daily companion — generous in scale, warm in material, forgiving of real life.

04
Kitchen

Chef's
Kitchen

Custom white oak cabinetry, honed stone island, brass pendants and a six-burner range. A kitchen scaled for serious cooking and effortless entertaining — the two are not mutually exclusive here.

05
Dining

The Dining
Room

A round oak table beneath a sculptural pendant, framed by floor-to-ceiling glass on two sides. Garden views at every meal. The kind of room that turns an ordinary Tuesday into something worth remembering.

Upper Level

Four private
retreats

Each bedroom was designed with the same intention as the primary — calm, considered, with its own relationship to light and garden. The kind of spaces that make guests reluctant to leave.

06
Primary Suite

A Private
Sanctuary

Four-poster bed, vaulted ceiling, morning light from the east and a view of the wetlands beyond. The bath is carved entirely from stone and Venetian plaster. A room in which time moves at its own pace — unhurried, quiet, restorative.

07
Upper Level

Light &
Volume

A sky-lit landing with glass balustrade opens to the floor below, flooding the centre of the house with natural light. One of those architectural moments that cannot be understood from a plan — only from standing in it.

08
Media Lounge

The
Lounge

Coffered ceiling, built-in oak cabinetry, deep seating. A space designed for long evenings — film, conversation, the quiet end of a Hamptons day. A room that rewards staying in.
